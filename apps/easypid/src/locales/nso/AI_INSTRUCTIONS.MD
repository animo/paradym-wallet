# Sepedi Translation Guidelines (nso)

## Overview
Sepedi (also known as Northern Sotho or Pedi) is a Sotho language spoken by approximately 4.7 million people in South Africa, primarily in Limpopo. This guide provides specific instructions for translating and maintaining Sepedi translations in the Paradym Wallet application.

## Tone & Style
- **Informal & Accessible**: Use "sa gago" for possessive pronouns
- **Direct Communication**: Prefer straightforward language structures
- **Respectful**: Use appropriate noun class prefixes (mo-/ba-, le-/di-, etc.)
- **Consistent**: Maintain uniform terminology throughout the app

## Grammatical Rules
- **Noun Classes**: Sepedi uses distinct noun class prefixes (mo-/ba-, le-/di-, mo-/me-, etc.)
- **Concordance**: Adjectives, verbs, and demonstratives must agree with noun class
- **Verbal Structure**: Verbs have specific subject and tense markers
- **Possessive Forms**: "sa gago" (your), "sa me" (my), etc.

## Common Phrases
- "Ke a fetoga" - I understand
- "Dumela" - Thank you / Agree
- "Ke ntshware" - I'm sorry
- "Ee" - Yes
- "Nnyaa" - No

## Placeholder Handling
- Placeholders {0}, {name}, {date} must remain unchanged in translations
- Keep placeholders in grammatically correct positions
- Example: "Karata ye go tswa go [name]" with placeholder intact

## Code Style
- Use UTF-8 encoding for all Sepedi characters
- Avoid complex HTML formatting
- Keep text concise for mobile display
